Fun With Pipes

Jan 12th, 2014

One of the things that attracts me and make me love Linux for development is the flexibility and the infinite amount of ways to solve a problem it gives you. The unix mantra says:

"Write programs that do one thing and do it well. Write programs to work together.
Write programs to handle text streams, because that is a universal interface."

One of the many tools this philosophy is plastered into is in names pipes. Named pipes are an easy way to provide intercomunication between different programs. While one program read from it, another one can write to it. Basically sending each other messages, communicating.

On Motivation

Aug 23rd, 2013

A demotivated non-engaging environment

Unless you’re a rock-star programmer or you run your own business, there are chances you don’t like how things get done where you work. If you’re a rock-star programmer you can easily choose where to work, you’ve trillion job offers at the most varied places. Things are different when you just started and can’t separate yourself from the crowd. You take jobs are chances to grow up. To learn and become a better professional.

Chances are you found a nice place to work, with a nice environment to grow and learn everyday, or not. You may end at a bloodsucking, soul crushing job where there is no feedback and you’re just another tool.
